It all goes deep into Statistics and Numbers

BIOLOG is having the mission to assess great quality of health services. Aiming to provide the best 'safety of care' and 'patient experience' with promising effectiveness and timeliness of care. All it started with a belief that all the flourishing technological advancements can somehow help people save their lives. Self promised to use the best of the advancements to achieve milestones in health care sector. Standing on that fine line between "poor quality health service access" and "non-utilization of existing health-care it takes a lot to make people fall into neither of the sides.

Health care funding is an important issue in developing countries like India. The health care sector is one of the India's largest public sectors in terms of revenue and employment with an approximate one fifth of public expenditure. In-spite of economic growth and demographic transition , the Indian health care system is burdened by a rise in infectious and and chronic degenerative diseases. It is has been established that the number of public health facilities in India is inadequate. A national study identified that the majority of the population (urban 46% and rural 36%) mainly use private doctors or clinics, with only 16% visiting public and private hospitals

The same study identified that urban Indians prefer private hospitals, while the rural population prefers public health care facilities. India's healthcare infrastructure has failed to keep pace with the nation's economic growth. Emerging market conditions and a poor public healthcare system has encouraged a shift from public to private healthcare , with private healthcare facilities now constituting more than 80% of healthcare expenditure, including that of those who are poor. Statistics found that the burden of such high expenditure on private healthcare had catastrophic effects on the household financial situation of those who are poor.

We also challenged ourselves to solve one of the most pronounced problem of developing countries " under-five mortality" It all left with a hope that timely access to health services might help them fight in the face of death.
